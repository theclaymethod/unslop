The bookshelf project began on a rainy Saturday. I wanted to repaint the old pine shelf and fix its wobble over one weekend.

Sanding came first. The old finish flaked off in long grey curls, and by lunchtime the bare wood was warm under my hand and smelled faintly of cut resin.

Two coats of soft grey went on next, each left to dry overnight, and the tired frame slowly turned into something I was glad to own. The pine and the paint together cost about $40.

Fixing the slack joints was the last job. Three screws, driven hard, pulled the four corners square.

My daughter claimed it that same evening. She loaded her paperbacks spine by spine and argued with me happily about their order until the last of the light went and we left the tallest stack leaning.
