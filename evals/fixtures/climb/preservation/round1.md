The bookshelf project began on a rainy Saturday. I wanted to repaint the old pine shelf and fix its wobble over one weekend.

The pine frame felt sturdy from the start, a solid base for the work ahead.

Sanding took most of the morning and stripped away years of grime.

The paint needed two coats to cover evenly, and I let each one dry overnight.

A handful of screws finally fixed the wobble and the loose joints held tight.

The bookshelf now stands steady, and that one weekend of pine paint and quiet repair felt entirely worth it.
