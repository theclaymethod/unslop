The bookshelf project began on a rainy Saturday. I wanted to repaint the old pine shelf and fix its wobble over one weekend.

Moreover, the pine frame felt sturdy from the start, offering a solid base for the work ahead, reinforcing my early confidence.

Furthermore, the sanding took most of the morning, stripping away years of grime, revealing the pale wood beneath.

However, the paint needed two coats to cover evenly, drying slowly between passes, testing whatever patience I had left.

In addition, three screws finally fixed the wobble, tightening the loose joints, stabilizing the whole piece at last.

Ultimately, the bookshelf project proved that one weekend and $40 of pine paint can rescue a tired old shelf.
