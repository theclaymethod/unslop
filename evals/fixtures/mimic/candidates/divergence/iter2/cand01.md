Aunt Rosa gave me a battered bookshelf. It had sat in her hall for years, and it wobbled hard whenever you tugged a book off of it. The pine was plain. It held its shape better than the costly stuff you see in shops, so I said I would take it home and fix it up over the weekend.

I sanded it first. The corners had flaked, and the grey dust got on my hands and my jeans and the floor. The work was slow. Slow and good, the way a chore with a clear end can be, where you see just what you did by dark. Then I gave it grey paint. A few screws took the wobble out, and the paint and the hardware came to about $40, which felt fair.

By dusk I stood back. The shelf was steady now, so I filled it with books and let the day go quiet.
