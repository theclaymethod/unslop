The company opened a repair hub beside its regional warehouse, allowing customers to return damaged units without shipping delays. Technicians now inspect each device within two business days, ensuring warranty decisions happen before replacements are ordered.

The hub also stocks common parts for older models, enabling repairs that stores previously sent to third-party shops. Managers added a shared queue for priority accounts, highlighting orders that affect hospitals and schools.

The first month reduced average turnaround from nine days to four days, demonstrating the value of local triage. Customer service teams now receive repair notes in the same system, reinforcing the link between diagnosis and follow-up.

The company plans to add weekend intake during the holiday season, paving the way for faster service during peak demand. Staff will review the pilot in January, contributing to the next regional support plan.
