The museum stayed open during the renovation by moving the entrance to the alley. Staff used temporary walls to keep dust away from the main gallery.

School tours changed first. Students began in the conservation lab, where they could see frames, solvents, and test patches before viewing the restored murals.

Weekend revenue held steady because the cafe had its own fire exit. Visitors still had a place to sit while crews worked on the front stairs.

By the end of the month, the construction route felt like part of the exhibit rather than an interruption.
