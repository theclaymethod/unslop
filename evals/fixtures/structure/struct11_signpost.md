This guide explains how a neighborhood pantry can prepare for summer demand without adding paid staff. First, the coordinator should count the number of households served in the last four weeks and compare it with the same period last year. In this section, the goal is to separate normal growth from a short-term spike.

Next, volunteers should check which shelves empty before Thursday afternoon. Having established the weekly pattern, the pantry can order more rice, beans, and diapers without overbuying canned goods that already move slowly.

In the following section, the pantry should assign drivers by route instead of by donor. As mentioned above, the constraint is volunteer time, so shorter routes matter more than perfect category sorting.

Let us turn to communication with partner churches and schools. In the next section, each partner receives one pickup window, one backup contact, and one list of items the pantry cannot store safely.
