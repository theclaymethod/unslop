Rain hit the bakery windows before sunrise.

Marta unlocked the side door, found three crates blocking the flour shelf, and laughed because the delivery driver had somehow stacked them around the broom like a monument.

The oven clicked.

By six, her brother was late, the card reader was dead, and a woman in a red coat was tapping on the glass with exact, theatrical patience.

Marta opened anyway. She sold two rolls for cash, wrote down four orders in pencil, and burned the first tray.

Then the power came back.

No one cheered; people just shifted closer to the counter, relieved that coffee would exist.

At nine, her brother arrived with a new reader and a story too long for the morning rush.
